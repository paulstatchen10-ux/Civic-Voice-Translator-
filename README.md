# Civic Voice Translator

**A free, open-source civic participation tool that translates any citizen's concern into three formats: plain English, structured public comment, and formal government/legal language — then helps route it to the right government body.**

Built for every citizen. Designed for the neighbor with no voice.

---

## Why This Exists

Public comment is a constitutional right. But the process is bureaucratic, time-consuming, and written in a language most citizens were never taught. Working people, unhoused neighbors, immigrants, non-English speakers, and people without political connections are systematically excluded — not because they don't care, but because the barrier to participation is too high.

This tool lowers that barrier as far as it can go.

You speak. It translates. You send it yourself.

The civic participation principle behind this tool is grounded in the United States naturalization oath and the constitutions of the United States, the State of California, and the principle of local self-governance from city to county to state to federal government.

---

## What It Does

Paste or speak any concern about your community — raw, fragmented, stream-of-consciousness. The tool produces three versions:

### ☀ Citizen Voice
Plain, clean, human English. Your voice preserved. Readable by any neighbor.

### ◈ Civic Comment
Structured for the public record. Includes:
- **Standing** — who you are and who you represent
- **Problem** — clearly defined in citizen terms
- **Recommendation** — a clear YES or NO with a one-sentence directive
- **Solution** — specific and actionable
- **Intended Outcome** — what success looks like
- **Unintended Consequences** — what to watch for (when applicable)

### ⚖ Government / Legal Language
Formal civic and legal language appropriate for submission to any California government body — Board of Supervisors, City Council, state agency, or federal office. Uses appropriate references to the Brown Act, CEQA, Government Code, municipal ordinance, and other applicable frameworks.

---

## Design Principles

- **The citizen always sends it themselves.** This tool drafts and routes. It never sends automatically. That is intentional and non-negotiable.
- **No political affiliation.** This tool serves civic participation, not any party, candidate, or ideology.
- **Not a spam tool.** Each output is a human-authored, AI-assisted civic communication. The citizen reviews and approves before anything goes anywhere.
- **Data sovereignty.** No data is stored. No accounts required. No tracking.
- **Jurisdiction-agnostic.** Designed for Santa Cruz County, California, but applicable to any city, county, state, or federal jurisdiction in the United States and adaptable internationally.

---

## Who It Speaks For

When a citizen uses this tool, they are encouraged to consider who they represent beyond themselves:

- Their immediate neighborhood
- Their city and county
- Their state and nation
- Those who cannot comment: working people without time, unhoused neighbors, immigrants, children, people with disabilities
- Those without legal standing: animals, ecosystems, future generations

This is the voice for those without a voice.
